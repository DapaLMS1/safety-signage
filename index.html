Poisons Sign

This sign warns that poisons or toxic chemicals are stored nearby. In a dental clinic, this might include disinfectants, sterilising chemicals or hazardous substances. Always follow safety procedures, read labels carefully, and wear the correct PPE.

Defibrillator Sign

This sign shows where the defibrillator is located. Dental assistants should know where it is and how to access it quickly in an emergency. Regular first aid and CPR training helps ensure you're prepared to respond to a medical emergency.

Biohazard Sign

This sign is used where contaminated waste is stored or processed, such as used sharps, blood-stained items, or PPE. Dental assistants must dispose of biohazard waste correctly to avoid the risk of infection or contamination.

First Aid Sign

This symbol shows the location of the first aid kit. In dental practices, kits should be stocked and accessible. Dental assistants must report any missing supplies and know where to find the kit during injuries or emergencies.

PPE Sign

This sign reminds staff and visitors that personal protective equipment (PPE) is required. In dental settings, PPE may include gloves, masks, gowns, and eye protection. Wearing the correct PPE reduces the risk of exposure to harmful substances.

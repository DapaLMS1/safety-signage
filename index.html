Different types of signs are used depending on the context. Safety signs can be found throughout the Dental Practice — for example, in the staffroom (techniques for washing hands), on equipment (warning labels), and in case of emergency (first aid kits and defibrillators).

Click on a safety sign to learn more.

Poisons Sign

This sign means hazardous or toxic substances are stored here. Dental clinics use cleaning chemicals and disinfectants that require proper handling. Dental assistants must follow correct procedures and use PPE to reduce risk.

Defibrillator Sign

This sign shows where to find a defibrillator. Dental staff must know where it is and how to use it in a medical emergency, such as a patient experiencing a heart attack.

Biohazard Sign

This indicates infectious or contaminated waste, such as blood-soiled materials, sharps, or PPE. It must be disposed of safely using the correct clinical waste process.

First Aid Sign

This sign shows where the first aid kit is located. As a dental assistant, it's important to regularly check the kit and restock it when needed. Quick access is essential during incidents.

PPE Sign

This sign reminds workers and visitors to wear personal protective equipment. In dental settings, this includes gloves, masks, gowns, and safety glasses. PPE helps prevent exposure to infections and harmful substances.
